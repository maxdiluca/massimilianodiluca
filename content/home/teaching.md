My teaching is research-led and interdisciplinary, helping students reason with models, data, technology, and evidence. I teach across computational cognitive science, psychophysics, human-computer interaction, extended reality, and perception, with an emphasis on reproducible methods and clear scientific explanation.

### Teaching

- Lead and develop the MSc module **Mind, Brain, and Models**, teaching students to simulate perceptual and cognitive systems, compare quantitative models, write reproducible code, and explain modelling choices in concise scientific reports.
- Contribute to postgraduate and interdisciplinary teaching in computational methods, psychophysics, HCI, VR, data science, and perception.
- Founded and directed the MSc in Computational Neuroscience and Cognitive Robotics, which later formed part of the path toward the MSc Computational Neuroscience and Data Science.
- Served as interim Director of MSc Psychology and as external examiner for postgraduate programmes at Durham University.
- Redesign assessment and teaching materials to support reproducibility, responsible AI use, technical confidence, and interpretation-focused scientific writing.

### Supervision

I supervise doctoral researchers, postdoctoral researchers, MSc students, undergraduate projects, internships, and volunteer placements across Psychology, Computer Science, computational neuroscience, HCI, XR, haptics, music interaction, and human-AI research.

My supervision record includes **50+ postgraduate taught and research projects** and **66+ undergraduate projects, internships, and volunteer placements**. Projects often connect students with live research activity through the [University of Birmingham VR Lab](https://virtualrealitylab.netlify.app/), [BhamXR](https://www.birmingham.ac.uk/research/centres-institutes/bhamxr), [ARME](https://arme-project.co.uk/), [EXPLORA](https://explora-network.github.io/web/index.html), and open-resource projects such as [Locomotion Vault](https://locomotionvault.github.io/) and [XR Text Trove](https://xrtexttrove.github.io/).

Former supervisees and project students have progressed to academic, technology, public-sector, and creative-industry roles, including Meta/Facebook, Google, Amazon, MIT, Disney/Ultraleap, the Office for National Statistics, the UN, Nottingham Trent University, McGill, University College Dublin, Otto-von-Guericke-Universitat Magdeburg, and the University of Stuttgart/LMU.

### Student Routes Into Research

Recent student-facing activity includes Research Volunteer Scheme projects in VR, computer vision, data annotation, and HCI gesture datasets; BSURE undergraduate research internships; Human-Computer Interaction outreach teaching for Birmingham school pupils; and student projects connected to immersive technologies for sustainable education.

### Student Applicants and VR Lab Roles

I welcome enquiries from students interested in PhD supervision, MSc and BSc projects, internships, research assistant roles, volunteering, and technically grounded XR project work. Current VR Lab routes include perception experiments, motion capture, haptics, accessibility, immersive interaction, AI-supported XR tools, and applied VR/AR/MR systems.

Students interested in lab-based roles should start with the [VR Lab opportunities page](https://virtualrealitylab.netlify.app/opportunities/), then get in touch through either [my contact page](#contact) or the [VR Lab contact page](https://virtualrealitylab.netlify.app/contact/). A useful first message includes a short description of your interests, relevant experience, availability, and the kind of research or technical role you would like to explore.
