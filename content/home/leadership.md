Di Luca leads the University of Birmingham VR Lab and founded and co-ordinates BhamXR, an interdisciplinary University network for extended reality, virtual worlds, haptics, AI-generated environments, telepresence, avatars, and spatial computing.

BhamXR is described by the University as an interdisciplinary network advancing immersive technologies and virtual worlds. Together with the VR Lab, ARME/JAMS, MyJAMS, and emerging mixed-reality infrastructure, this work creates shared platforms for research, training, public engagement, and industry-facing collaboration.

The programme connects perception science with deployable XR systems: measuring latency and synchrony, modelling multisensory integration, designing haptic and embodied interaction, and translating research outputs into open resources, datasets, demonstrators, and software.
