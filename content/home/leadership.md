Di Luca leads the [University of Birmingham VR Lab](https://virtualrealitylab.netlify.app/) and founded and co-ordinates [BhamXR](https://www.birmingham.ac.uk/research/centres-institutes/bhamxr), an interdisciplinary University network for extended reality, virtual worlds, haptics, AI-generated environments, telepresence, avatars, and spatial computing.

[BhamXR](https://www.birmingham.ac.uk/research/centres-institutes/bhamxr) is described by the University as an interdisciplinary network advancing immersive technologies and virtual worlds. Together with the [VR Lab](https://virtualrealitylab.netlify.app/), [ARME](https://arme-project.co.uk/), [MyJAMS](https://myjamsltd.netlify.app/), and emerging mixed-reality infrastructure, this work creates shared platforms for research, training, public engagement, and industry-facing collaboration.

The programme connects perception science with deployable XR systems: measuring latency and synchrony, modelling multisensory integration, designing haptic and embodied interaction, and translating research outputs into open resources, datasets, demonstrators, and software.
